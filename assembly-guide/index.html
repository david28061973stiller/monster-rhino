Before You Start.

This is the assembly guide for the Monster Rhino Kitbase.

The project is based on the work of valued community member @TheAmazinGreat. It is furthermore based on the original DIY Rhino project by @protomaker964.

Tools

What You Need.

  • A set of Allen keys or a ratchet with suitable bits
  • A set of Torx keys or a ratchet with suitable bits
  • An electrician's slotted screwdriver
  • A wire-end ferrule crimper, if you do not already have one. All wires supplied with the Monster Kitbase are pre-crimped. The power wires supplied with the VPForce motor kit come without ferrules. It is highly recommended that they be fitted with ferrules.

Non-binding recommendation

Power Supply Guidance.

Solo Device

Monster Rhino Only.

For a standalone Monster Rhino, a Mean Well LRS-350-24 or a technically comparable power supply is recommended. Installation, enclosure, wiring, grounding and protection must comply with the power-supply manufacturer's instructions and all applicable electrical requirements.

Multiple Devices

Rhino + FFB Pedals.

For setups powering multiple devices, such as a Monster Rhino together with VPForce-modified FFB pedals, select a power supply rated for the combined expected continuous load and peak demand. Use the current official VPForce documentation when sizing and wiring the supply.

95% preassembled

Final Assembly.

Complete the steps in order. Keep all supplied screws and cable ties within reach.

01

Access the housing

Remove the marked covers.

Remove the bottom plate, the front cover with the intake fan, and both belt covers. Every part that must be removed is marked with a sticker. For transport, the covers are secured with only two or three screws; put all parts and screws aside safely.

Detach the emergency stop. Press in the white latch on its lower section and pull the lower section down.
02

Install the electronics

Mount the mainboard.

Locate the mainboard bracket beneath the front cover and remove the four preinstalled mounting screws. Position the mainboard on the bracket and secure it with those four screws.

Check the orientation. Align the board so the power-wire connectors point to the right, matching the orientation shown in Fig. 1.
03

Motor orientation and wiring

Install the motors and connect the mainboard.

  1. Prepare the motor cables first. Attach and crimp the supplied cables before placing the motors in the housing. The connectors cannot be reached after installation.
  2. Install both motors with the supplied M6 motor-mount screws. Match the orientation shown in Fig. 2 so the cables do not contact the outer walls when belt tension is adjusted.
  3. Connect the electronics according to Fig. 1. The two fan plugs and the two potentiometer plugs are interchangeable.
  4. Route every cable clear of both fans and all moving parts. Once wiring is complete, ensure the emergency stop is released.
!

Polarity is critical.

Reversed motor-power polarity can permanently damage the electronics. Follow the plus and minus labels on the kit. If you shorten uncrimped power cables, fit wire-end ferrules for a reliable connection.

04

Test before closing

Verify, tidy and close the housing.

Power the base only after checking the wiring. Confirm that the electronics work and test both fans in the VPForce software's Debug tab. Disconnect power again before closing the case.

  • Secure the wiring with the supplied cable ties.
  • Keep every cable clear of the front intake fan.
  • Install the vertical reinforcers shown in Fig. 3.
  • Refit both covers and tighten all remaining case screws.
05

Drive pulleys

Fit the 15T metal pulleys.

Slide a supplied 15T pulley onto each motor shaft. Align each grub screw with a flattened face of the shaft, then tighten it. Apply a suitable threadlocker to prevent the screws loosening and the pulley slipping.

Do not push the pulley too far down. Leave enough adjustment range to align both belts. If the fit is tight, use only a plastic or rubber mallet and work gently.

Mechanical setup

Belts & Calibration.

Center first, tension carefully, then let the software compensate for the final offset.

  1. 01
    Check the starting value.

    Power on the base and open the RAW readings in the VPForce software. Both should be approximately 2000.

  2. 02
    Install the first belt.

    Hold the grip connector centered while fitting the belt. Keep the software dot centered and the reading close to 2000. If needed, move the belt by one tooth.

  3. 03
    Set the tension.

    The belt should be firm, but never as tight as a guitar string. Do not overtighten.

  4. 04
    Repeat for the second axis.

    Install and tension the second belt using the same method.

  5. 05
    Run calibration.

    Perform the calibration described on page 16 of the VPForce manual. An exact initial RAW value of 2000 is not required; calibration compensates for the offset.

  6. 06
    Tune the effects.

    Add spring force, damper, inertia and other effects to preference. Refer to the VPForce manual for detailed guidance.

Ready for the rig

Final Checks.

01

Select the grip.

Choose the correct grip in the VPForce Configurator. VPForce offers adapters for VKB grips (separate BlackBox required) and Winwing grips.

02

Check low-throw limiters.

The Monster Rhino Kitbase comes preinstalled with ±20° throw limiters and is compatible with the original Rhino throw limiters. If required, these can be customised using the Onshape file provided by VPForce and 3D-printed locally. With extremely low limiters, ensure the front grip cable does not rub. If necessary, raise the limiter a few millimetres using washers.

03

Mount and fly.

Securely mount the base to your rig, complete one final movement and cable-clearance check, and enjoy the flight.
